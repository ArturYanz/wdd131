What is an Orchestra?

An orchestra is a large musical ensemble composed of multiple instrument families, including strings, woodwinds, brass and percussion. Each section has a distinct role in shaping the music's harmony, rhythm, and dynamics. Guided by a conductor, the orchestra performs complex compositions that combine emotion, structure, and artistic expression.
